Aesthetics is paramount when it comes to solar. Our roof-integrated solar products have superior water-shedding qualities. And they’re installed by those who know roofs best, the DFW area over: Falcon DFW Solar.

Sleek, low-profile, and precision-built, these are the roofs your neighbors will envy.

Solar pays for the roof

Solar can produce electricity at a lower cost than your utility company. The more you produce and use your own electricity, the less you purchase. Over time, your solar energy may even pay for itself AND your new roof, via monthly electric bill savings and government incentives.

You may also get credit for solar energy you send back to the grid when your panels produce more power than your home uses. This could further increase your monthly savings.

Of course, savings will vary and are not guaranteed. Incentives and eligibility requirements vary and availability is not guaranteed.

Our Process

6 Steps to Power On

1

Home Assessment

Once you provide details about your home, we remotely design your system

Complete your self-survey
Upload your electric bill and take some photos of your home

We design your system
Based on satellite imagery and your self-survey, we remotely design your system

2

Third Party Approval

We make sure third-party approvals are in place from your local permitting authority and/or utility. If you have a Homeowners Association (HOA), you may need to obtain additional approval of the design.

3

Installation

Once third-party approvals are received, we schedule the first available install date, which can be rescheduled at any time

4

Inspection

We coordinate inspection with your local authority

5

Utility Approval

We secure permission from the utility to connect your system to the grid
